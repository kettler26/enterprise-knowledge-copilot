# GTM Pilot Playbook (SaaS)

This playbook turns the product into a repeatable commercial motion for SaaS customers.

## 1) Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Primary segment:

- B2B SaaS companies, 20-500 employees
- 5-100 support agents
- 1,000-50,000 monthly support tickets
- Existing documentation sprawl (Notion, Confluence, Zendesk KB, Google Docs)

Economic buyer:

- Head of Support, VP CX, COO

Technical champion:

- Support Operations Manager, IT Lead

## 2) Problem Statement You Sell

Pitch in one sentence:

- "We reduce support workload and response time by grounding AI replies in your internal knowledge and ticket context, with full traceability."

Pain checklist:

- repeated tickets consume high-cost human time
- inconsistent answers across agents
- long ramp-up for new support reps
- weak auditability of AI outputs

## 3) Commercial Offer (Pilot)

Pilot duration:

- 6 weeks

Pilot scope:

- 1 business unit
- 1 ticketing integration
- 1 documentation source
- read-first mode, controlled write in final phase

Pilot pricing template:

- setup: EUR 3,000-8,000 one-time
- pilot fee: EUR 1,500-4,000 per month
- conversion credit: up to 50 percent of setup fee applied to annual contract

## 4) Baseline and KPI Targets

Collect baseline in week 0:

- average handle time (AHT)
- first response time (FRT)
- ticket deflection rate
- escalation rate
- CSAT

Target ranges for successful pilot:

- AHT improvement: 15-30 percent
- FRT improvement: 20-40 percent
- deflection: 10-25 percent on repetitive categories
- onboarding time reduction for new reps: 25+ percent

## 5) ROI Formula for Buyer Deck

Simple ROI model:

- monthly savings = (tickets automated or shortened) x (minutes saved per ticket) x (loaded hourly cost / 60)
- annual ROI = (12 x monthly savings) - annual software cost

Decision rule:

- if projected payback <= 6 months, move to annual contract

## 6) Pilot Delivery Plan (6 weeks)

Week 1:

- integration setup
- baseline KPI snapshot
- security and access controls

Week 2-3:

- retrieval quality tuning
- prompt and policy guardrails
- live shadow mode with support team

Week 4-5:

- controlled production usage
- weekly KPI review with buyer and champion

Week 6:

- final KPI report
- rollout plan
- commercial close for annual subscription

## 7) Security and Trust Requirements

Minimum controls for enterprise acceptance:

- role-based access
- per-workspace data separation
- full trace logs for prompts, tools, and outputs
- secret management and key rotation policy

## 8) Conversion Criteria (Pilot -> Paid)

Pilot converts when:

- at least 2 of 3 core KPIs improve beyond target threshold
- support leadership confirms workflow fit
- no unresolved critical security concerns

If not converted:

- capture blocker category (integration, quality, trust, change management)
- map blocker to a remediation sprint
- re-offer a time-boxed extension only with explicit success metric

## 9) Packaging for Annual Contract

Plan structure:

- Starter: one workspace, core RAG + draft assist
- Growth: multi-workspace, analytics, advanced connectors
- Enterprise: SSO/SAML, custom SLA, dedicated environment, audit exports

Recommended annual floor:

- EUR 12k-25k ARR for first paid account in this ICP

## 10) First 20 Customer Outreach List

Prioritize:

- VC-backed B2B SaaS with growing support load
- companies hiring support ops roles
- teams already using Zendesk/Intercom + Notion/Confluence

Outbound angle:

- free KPI baseline workshop
- paid pilot with clear success thresholds
